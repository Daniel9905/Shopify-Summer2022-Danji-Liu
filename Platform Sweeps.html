BACKGROUND

Currently, 3 internal teams at Wish sweep 290 keywords and address 804 listings per day. In contrast, the e-commerce marketplace reaches 200 million products. The growth overpowers the staggering review speed.

USER PROBLEM

QUALITATIVE INSIGHTS

Pain-points in current workflow

Limited location filter

2 extra steps to change location by updating local address. Limited to 1 country. No other filters.

Lack of speed

There're 8 steps to review 1 keyword per location.

Hard to access merchant info

There're 2 more pages to access merchant info. Go to the merchant page and log onto the merchant portal.

DESIGN UPDATES

Before

Most links are surfaced as a backup. However, 80% of participants (4/5) clicked on link to review products. 100% (5/5) attempted to copy PID into excel sheet, defaulting back to the old workflow.

After

We decided to hide the backup links (Copy PID, View on Wish) into second-level menu to encourage engagement and enforce workflow shift. Reviews link is singled out because of its priority in agents' decision making

Before

3/5 participants struggled with completing keyword. Challenge is to emphasize completion without changing the priority of browsing products.

After

Regroup the headers. Search header is grouped with search results to increase context. Keyword statistics are moved to the left to increase discoverability.

After

3/5 participants reported feelings of intimidation and lack of trust when they started using the tool. One way to establish clarity with users is to give contextualized instructions when they first use the tool

REFLECTION

Learnings

Be an advocate for users

As a designer, I constantly found myself defending user needs, especially in a PM-driven environment. This requires me to do extensive research before designing and to keep bringing the user mental model into conversations with other stakeholders.

Share often and early

I signed up for two design critiques where I received critical help both times. I learned how to communicate with other talented design team members, whether it be presentation or verbal walkthrough, to address my design issues.

Design within and beyond the eco-system

This is my first time navigating a large and yet still evolving design system. I learned to update my design in compliance with the system but also gave feedback to improve the components.

Over-communicate with manager

My first engineering sync session went poorly as we digressed from engineering feasibility to visual critique. It was only when I talked to my manager that I realized I could've over-communicated with them to ask for tips, timeline, and planning to avoid similar situations.
